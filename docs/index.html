Course Overview

This course provides an introduction to important topics in statistical concepts and reasoning essential to quantitative medical research. This course is lectured by a total of three faculty:

  • Week 1 to 5: Dr. Boncho Ku
  • Week 6 to 9: Dr. Mimi Ko
  • Week 10 to 14: Dr. Myeong Soo Lee

Course Level

Introductory to intermediate

Prerequisites

None. However, we expect that students are familiar with basic statistics.

Weekly Topics

Course Schedule (Week 1 ~ Week 9)
Week Description Lecturer
Week 1 (2021.03.04) Introduction Dr. Boncho Ku
Week 2 (2021.03.11) Terminologies and descriptive statistics Dr. Boncho Ku
Week 3 (2021.03.18) Probability, expectation, and Important probability distributions Dr. Boncho Ku
Week 4 (2021.03.25) Random variables, sampling distribution and central limit theorem Dr. Boncho Ku
Week 5 (2021.04.01) Estimation and hypothesis testing Dr. Boncho Ku
Week 6 (2021.04.08) Analysis of continuous data (part 1) Dr. Mimi Ko
Week 7 (2021.04.15) Analysis of continuous data (part 2) Dr. Mimi Ko
Week 8 (2021.04.22) Analysis of categorical data Dr. Mimi Ko
Week 9 (2021.04.29) More applications (sample size calculation, regression analysis) Dr. Mimi Ko

Materials

  1. Study Design and Statistical Analysis : A Practical Guide for Clinicians. Mitchell H. Katz. 2006. Cambridge University Press
  2. Medical Statistics: A Guide to SPSS, Data Analysis and Critical Appraisal. Berlinda Barton and Jennifer Peat. 2nd Edition. 2014. WILEY Blackwell
  3. Statistics: Concepts and Controversies. David S. Moore and Willian Notz. 10th Edition. 2020. W.H. Freeman
  4. Introduction to Statistical Methodology. Derek L. Sonderegger and Robert Buscaglia. https://bookdown.org/dereksonderegger/570/
